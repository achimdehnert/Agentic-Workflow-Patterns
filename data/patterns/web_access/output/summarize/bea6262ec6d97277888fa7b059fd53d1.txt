# Angelina Jolie: Actress, Filmmaker, and Humanitarian

Angelina Jolie is a renowned American actress, filmmaker, and humanitarian. She has received numerous accolades throughout her career, including an Academy Award, a Tony Award, and three Golden Globe Awards. Jolie's film career began in the early 1990s and gained significant momentum with her performance in the 1999 drama *Girl, Interrupted*, which earned her an Oscar for Best Supporting Actress. She became a household name after starring as the iconic Lara Croft in *Lara Croft: Tomb Raider* (2001) and has starred in many action films since, including *Mr. & Mrs. Smith* (2005), *Wanted* (2008), and *Salt* (2010). Jolie has also received acclaim for her roles in dramas like *A Mighty Heart* (2007) and *Changeling* (2008), which earned her a nomination for the Academy Award for Best Actress. 

Jolie has transitioned into directing and writing, with her directorial credits including the war dramas *In the Land of Blood and Honey* (2011), *Unbroken* (2014), and *First They Killed My Father* (2017), and the musical *The Outsiders* (2024), which won the Tony Award for Best Musical. 

Beyond her acting and filmmaking career, Jolie is known for her significant contributions to humanitarian work. She has been a Special Envoy for the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) since 2012, promoting conservation, education, and women's rights.  She has undertaken numerous field missions to refugee camps and war zones worldwide, raising awareness for their plight. In 2013, she received the Jean Hersholt Humanitarian Award, an honorary Academy Award, for her contributions to humanitarian causes. 

Jolie has been recognized as one of the most powerful and influential figures in the American entertainment industry. She has been repeatedly named the world's most beautiful woman by various publications. Her personal life, including her relationships, marriages, and family, has also garnered significant attention. Jolie has been married three times, to actors Jonny Lee Miller, Billy Bob Thornton, and Brad Pitt.  She has six children with Pitt, three of whom were adopted internationally.
