# Meryl Streep: A Career Defined by Versatility and Acclaim

Meryl Streep is an American actress known for her exceptional versatility and ability to convincingly portray a wide range of characters. Her career spans over four decades and includes numerous accolades, including a record-breaking 21 Academy Award nominations, winning three, and 34 Golden Globe nominations, winning eight. 

Streep made her stage debut in 1975 and received a Tony Award nomination for Best Featured Actress in a Play for a double-bill production of 27 Wagons Full of Cotton and A Memory of Two Mondays. Her feature film debut came in Julia (1977), and she received her first Oscar nomination for The Deer Hunter (1978). 

Streep gained critical acclaim for her work in the 1980s, including an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress for her performance in Kramer vs. Kramer (1979), followed by the Academy Award for Best Actress for her role as a Holocaust survivor in Sophie's Choice (1982). 

Her successful film career continued throughout the 1990s, with films like Out of Africa (1985), Death Becomes Her (1992), and The Bridges of Madison County (1995) earning significant commercial success. 

Streep's popularity and acclaim have continued in the ensuing decades with leading roles in The Devil Wears Prada (2006), Doubt (2008), Mamma Mia! (2008), Julie & Julia (2009), and It's Complicated (2009). 

She won her third Oscar for her portrayal of Margaret Thatcher in The Iron Lady (2011). For her work on television, she won three Primetime Emmy Awards for her roles in the miniseries Holocaust (1978) and Angels in America (2003), as well as the narration in the documentary series Five Came Back (2017).

In 2023, Streep joined the cast of Hulu's comedy series Only Murders in the Building for its third season, earning an Emmy nomination for her performance.

Streep has also been the recipient of many honorary awards, including the AFI Life Achievement Award in 2004, a Gala Tribute from the Film Society of Lincoln Center in 2008, and the Kennedy Center Honor in 2011. President Barack Obama awarded her the National Medal of Arts in 2010 and the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2014.

- Meryl Streep is considered one of the most versatile and acclaimed actresses of her generation. 
- She has received more Oscar and Golden Globe nominations than any other actor in history.
- Streep is known for her ability to convincingly portray a wide range of characters, often mastering accents for her roles. 
- She is a successful actress both in film and on television. 
- Streep has also been recognized for her humanitarian and political work.
