# Robert Downey Jr. Information

Robert Downey Jr. is an American actor known for his roles in various films including Iron Man, The Avengers, and Sherlock Holmes. He was born on April 4, 1965, in Manhattan, New York City. Downey's career has been characterized by early success, a period of drug-related problems and legal issues, and a surge in popularity and commercial success since the late 2000s. His films have grossed over $14 billion worldwide, making him one of the highest-grossing actors of all time. 

Downey began his acting career at the age of five, appearing in his father's film Pound. He later worked with the Brat Pack in teen films such as Weird Science and Less Than Zero. His portrayal of Charlie Chaplin in the 1992 biopic Chaplin earned him a BAFTA Award and an Academy Award nomination. Downey's struggles with drug addiction began in his youth and continued throughout the 1990s, leading to several arrests and stints in rehab. He eventually achieved sobriety in 2003 and made a successful comeback. 

Downey's breakthrough role came in 2008 with Iron Man, where he played Tony Stark, a billionaire inventor with a superhero alter ego. The film was a critical and commercial success, launching the Marvel Cinematic Universe. He has since reprised the role in nine more MCU films, including The Avengers series. He also played Sherlock Holmes in Guy Ritchie's Sherlock Holmes films. In 2023, Downey won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his performance as Lewis Strauss in Christopher Nolan's Oppenheimer. He is known for his versatility, his ability to portray both comedic and dramatic roles, and his charming personality. He is also a successful producer, co-founding Team Downey with his wife, Susan Levin, and has also launched a coffee company named Happy.
