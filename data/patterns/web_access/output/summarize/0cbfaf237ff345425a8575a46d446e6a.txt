# Tom Hanks: A Look at America's Dad

Tom Hanks is one of the most recognizable and beloved actors in Hollywood, known for his comedic and dramatic roles. He has starred in a vast array of films, including comedies like *Splash* (1984) and *Big* (1988), and dramas like *Philadelphia* (1993) and *Forrest Gump* (1994). Hanks is the only actor to have won consecutive Academy Awards for Best Actor, achieving this feat in 1993 and 1994. 

Hanks has frequently collaborated with directors such as Steven Spielberg, Ron Howard, and Nora Ephron. He is also known for his voice acting roles, most notably as Sheriff Woody in the *Toy Story* films (1995-2019).

Hanks' films have grossed over $9.96 billion worldwide, making him the fourth-highest-grossing American actor. He has been nominated for six Academy Awards, winning two for Best Actor and one for Best Supporting Actor. 

He made his Broadway debut in 2013 in Nora Ephron's *Lucky Guy*, earning a Tony Award nomination. Hanks has also been honored with the AFI Life Achievement Award (2002), the Kennedy Center Honor (2014), the Presidential Medal of Freedom (2016), and the French Legion of Honor (2016). 

Hanks' early career was marked by a series of comedic roles, but he quickly transitioned into more dramatic fare. He has demonstrated a remarkable ability to portray a wide range of characters, from the endearing and lovable to the complex and flawed. 

He is often referred to as "America's Dad" due to his wholesome persona and relatable characters. Hanks' approachable and charismatic demeanor has made him a cultural icon, and his work continues to resonate with audiences worldwide.
