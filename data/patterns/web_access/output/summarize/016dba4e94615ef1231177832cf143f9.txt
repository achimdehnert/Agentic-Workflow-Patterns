# Viola Davis: A Cinematic Icon

Viola Davis is an American actress and film producer who has achieved both critical acclaim and commercial success. She is renowned for her work across screen and stage, earning her the prestigious Triple Crown of Acting and the EGOT. 

Davis's journey began in Central Falls, Rhode Island, where she faced poverty and dysfunction.  She developed a love for acting at a young age and pursued theater at Rhode Island College before graduating from the prestigious Juilliard School.  She made her Broadway debut in August Wilson's "Seven Guitars" in 1996, a performance that garnered critical acclaim and a Tony Award nomination. 

Davis continued to make her mark on both stage and screen throughout the 2000s.  She won a Tony Award for her portrayal of Tonya in August Wilson's "King Hedley II" in 2001 and a second Tony Award for her role as Rose Maxson in the Broadway revival of Wilson's "Fences" in 2010.  She received her first Academy Award nomination for her performance in "Doubt" (2008).

Davis is a vocal advocate for human rights and women of color.  She has donated funds to her hometown public library and her alma mater's theater program.  She is also a partner in the Hunger Is campaign to help eradicate childhood hunger in America. 

Davis's memoir, "Finding Me," was published in 2022 and she won a Grammy Award for the audio version of the book. She continues to be a leading figure in the entertainment industry, earning a reputation for her powerful performances and dedication to social justice.
