# Viola Davis: Award-Winning Actress and Activist

Viola Davis is an American actress, producer, and New York Times best-selling author known for her impactful performances across screen and stage.  She is recognized for her precise, controlled performances and regal presence. [1] 

Davis was born in St. Matthews, South Carolina on August 11, 1965, and grew up in Central Falls, Rhode Island, where she faced poverty and hardship. [2, 4] Her acting career began in school productions, and she continued to study theater at Rhode Island College and the Juilliard School. [1, 2]

She made her Broadway debut in 1996 in August Wilson's *Seven Guitars* and received her first Tony Award nomination. [1, 2] She won her first Tony for her role in Wilson's *King Hedley II* in 2001. [1, 2]  She has since won two Tony Awards, three Drama Desk Awards, an Obie Award, and a Theater World Award. [1]  

Davis has appeared in numerous films, including *Doubt* (2008), *The Help* (2011), and *Fences* (2016), for which she received multiple nominations and awards. [1, 2]  She won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress for her role in *Fences* in 2016, making her the first black actress to achieve the Triple Crown of Acting by winning an Oscar, Emmy, and Tony. [1, 2]

Davis is a strong advocate for human rights and women of color. [1] She has donated funds to her hometown library and her high school's theater program, and has collaborated with the Hunger Is campaign to help eradicate childhood hunger. [1] 

In 2022, Davis became the 18th person to achieve EGOT status by winning a Grammy Award for Best Audio Book, Narration & Storytelling Recording for the narration of her 2022 autobiography *Finding Me*. [1]
