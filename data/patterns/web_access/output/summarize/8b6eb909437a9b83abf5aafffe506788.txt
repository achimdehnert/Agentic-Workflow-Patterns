# Jennifer Lawrence: Actress, Producer, and Advocate

Jennifer Lawrence is an American actress and producer known for her versatile roles in both action franchises and independent dramas. Born on August 15, 1990, in Indian Hills, Kentucky, Lawrence discovered her passion for acting at a young age and began performing in school plays and local theater. She made her film debut in 2008 and gained international recognition for her roles in the X-Men and The Hunger Games franchises. Lawrence is also known for her critically acclaimed performances in films like "Silver Linings Playbook" and "American Hustle." 

Lawrence has won numerous awards for her acting, including an Academy Award, a Golden Globe Award, and a BAFTA Award. She is also a vocal advocate for women's rights and has spoken out against the gender pay gap in Hollywood. Lawrence is a strong supporter of various charities, including the Boys & Girls Clubs of America and the Special Olympics. In 2018, she co-founded the Time's Up initiative to combat sexual harassment and discrimination in Hollywood. 

Lawrence's career has been marked by both critical and commercial success. Her films have grossed over $6 billion worldwide. She has also been recognized as the highest-grossing action heroine of all time. She has recently starred in "Don't Look Up," "Causeway," and "No Hard Feelings," continuing to explore diverse roles and genres. Lawrence's personal life has also been a source of media attention, and she is married to art dealer Cooke Maroney.
