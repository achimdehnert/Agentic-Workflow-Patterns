# Scarlett Johansson: A Multifaceted Career

Scarlett Johansson, born November 22, 1984, is an American actress and singer who has achieved global recognition for her diverse roles across various genres. [1, 2] She began acting at a young age, appearing in off-Broadway plays and films like "North" (1994) and "Manny & Lo" (1996). [1, 2, 3] Her breakthrough role came in 2003 with "Lost in Translation," which earned her a BAFTA Award for Best Actress. [1, 4] She continued to showcase her range in films such as "Girl with a Pearl Earring" (2003), "Match Point" (2005), and "Vicky Cristina Barcelona" (2008), solidifying her position as a versatile actress. [1, 2, 3]

Johansson's career took a significant turn in 2010 when she joined the Marvel Cinematic Universe as Black Widow in "Iron Man 2." [1, 3] She reprised the role in eight films, including "The Avengers," "Captain America: The Winter Soldier," and "Black Widow" (2021). [1, 3] These roles propelled her to international fame and established her as a box-office draw. [1, 3, 4]

Beyond Marvel, Johansson has starred in critically acclaimed films like "Her" (2013), "Under the Skin" (2013), and "Lucy" (2014). [1, 3, 4] In 2019, she received two Academy Award nominations for her performances in "Marriage Story" and "Jojo Rabbit," becoming one of the few actors to achieve this feat. [1, 4] 

Johansson has also ventured into music, releasing her debut album "Anywhere I Lay My Head" (2008), featuring covers of Tom Waits songs. [1, 2, 3] She has also collaborated with singer-songwriter Pete Yorn on two albums, "Break Up" (2009) and "Apart" (2018). [2, 3]

Outside of acting and music, Johansson is a prominent brand endorser and a vocal advocate for various charitable causes. [1] She has been a global ambassador for Oxfam and has supported organizations like Stand Up To Cancer and Too Many Women. [1] She has also been active in political campaigns, endorsing Democratic candidates such as John Kerry, Barack Obama, and Hillary Clinton. [1]

Johansson's personal life has been the subject of media attention, with her marriages to Ryan Reynolds (2008-2011), Romain Dauriac (2014-2017), and comedian Colin Jost (2020) drawing public scrutiny. [1, 3, 4]
