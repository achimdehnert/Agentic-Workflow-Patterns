# Leonardo DiCaprio: A Life in Film and Activism

Leonardo DiCaprio is an American actor and film producer known for his diverse range of roles, particularly in biographical and period films. [1]  He began acting as a child, appearing in television commercials and later landing roles in TV series like "Parenthood" and "Growing Pains". [2, 3] DiCaprio achieved international fame with the star-crossed romances "Romeo + Juliet" (1996) and "Titanic" (1997). [1, 2]  However, he sought to move beyond his teen idol image and explore more complex characters, resulting in critically acclaimed performances in films like "Catch Me If You Can" (2002), "Gangs of New York" (2002), and "The Aviator" (2004). [1, 2]

DiCaprio has been recognized by the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS) for his performances in "What's Eating Gilbert Grape" (1993), "The Aviator", "Blood Diamond" (2006), "The Wolf of Wall Street" (2013), "The Revenant" (2015), and "Once Upon a Time in Hollywood" (2019). [1]  He won the Academy Award for Best Actor for "The Revenant" and has won three Golden Globe Awards for his performances in "The Aviator," "The Revenant," and "The Wolf of Wall Street." [1, 2] 

In addition to his successful acting career, DiCaprio is a passionate advocate for environmentalism and climate change awareness. [1, 2] He founded the Leonardo DiCaprio Foundation in 1998 to support organizations and campaigns dedicated to preserving the environment. [1, 2] DiCaprio has served as a United Nations Messenger of Peace, urging world leaders to address climate change. [1] He has produced several documentaries on environmental issues, including "The 11th Hour" (2007) and "Before the Flood" (2016), and he has used his platform to raise awareness about the urgency of climate action. [1, 2]
