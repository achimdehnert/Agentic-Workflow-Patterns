# Scarlett Johansson: A Multifaceted Star

Scarlett Johansson is an acclaimed American actress and singer whose impressive career spans decades.  Her versatility has led to critical acclaim in various genres, from period dramas and thrillers to science fiction and action-adventure films.  

Johansson began her acting career as a child, appearing in off-Broadway productions and films like "North" (1994) and "Manny & Lo" (1996).  She gained wider recognition for her performance in "The Horse Whisperer" (1998) and achieved mainstream success with her role in "Lost in Translation" (2003), which earned her a BAFTA Award for Best Actress. 

Throughout her career, Johansson has collaborated with renowned directors, including Woody Allen, Sofia Coppola, and Christopher Nolan.  She has starred in critically acclaimed films like "Girl with a Pearl Earring" (2003), "Match Point" (2005), "The Prestige" (2006), and "Vicky Cristina Barcelona" (2008).  

She is also known for her role as Black Widow in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), a character she has portrayed in eight films, including "Iron Man 2" (2010), "The Avengers" (2012), and "Black Widow" (2021).  

In 2019, Johansson received two Academy Award nominations for her roles in "Marriage Story" and "Jojo Rabbit." She has also won a Tony Award for Best Featured Actress for her performance in "A View from the Bridge" on Broadway.  

Beyond acting, Johansson has pursued a successful music career.  She released her debut album, "Anywhere I Lay My Head," in 2008, and followed it with a collaborative album with singer-songwriter Pete Yorn titled "Break Up" in 2009.

Johansson has been a prominent brand endorser and has supported various charitable causes, including Oxfam and Stand Up To Cancer.
