# Denzel Washington: A Career Overview

Denzel Washington is a renowned American actor, producer, and director. He is known for his powerful performances spanning over four decades, earning recognition as one of the greatest actors of his generation.  Washington has received numerous accolades including two Academy Awards, a Tony Award, three Golden Globe Awards, a Screen Actors Guild Award and two Silver Bears.

Washington's career began on stage, performing off-Broadway. He achieved prominence in the 1980s with his role as Dr. Phillip Chandler in the NBC medical drama series _St. Elsewhere_ and for his performance in the war film _A Soldier's Story_. In 1989, he won his first Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his role in the war drama _Glory_. Washington has been nominated for numerous Oscars, including for his performances in _Cry Freedom_ (1987), _Malcolm X_ (1992), _The Hurricane_ (1999), _Flight_ (2012), _Fences_ (2016), _Roman J. Israel, Esq._ (2017), and _The Tragedy of Macbeth_ (2021).

He has starred in a wide range of films including _Mo' Better Blues_ (1990), _Mississippi Masala_ (1991), _Philadelphia_ (1993), _Courage Under Fire_ (1996), _Remember the Titans_ (2000), _Man on Fire_ (2004), _Inside Man_ (2006), _American Gangster_ (2007), and _The Equalizer_ trilogy (2014-2023). Washington has also directed and starred in films like _Antwone Fisher_ (2002), _The Great Debaters_ (2007), and _Fences_ (2016). 

He has consistently achieved critical acclaim for his portrayals of real-life figures including Steve Biko in _Cry Freedom_, Malcolm X in _Malcolm X_, Rubin 'Hurricane' Carter in _The Hurricane_, Melvin B. Tolson in _The Great Debaters_, Frank Lucas in _American Gangster_, and Coach Herman Boone in _Remember the Titans_. Washington won his second Academy Award for Best Actor in 2001 for his role in the crime thriller _Training Day_. 

Washington has returned to Broadway in the revivals of _Checkmates_ (1988), _Julius Caesar_ (2005), _August Wilson's Fences_ (2010), _Lorraine Hansberry's A Raisin in the Sun_ (2014) and _Eugene O'Neill's The Iceman Cometh_ (2018). He won the Tony Award for Best Actor in a Play for starring in the Broadway revival of _August Wilson's Fences_ in 2010. He later directed, produced, and starred in the film adaptation in 2016.
