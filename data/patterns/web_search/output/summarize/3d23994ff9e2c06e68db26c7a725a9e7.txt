# Robert Downey Jr. Information

Robert Downey Jr. is an American actor known for his versatile roles and box office success. His career spans over four decades, marked by early success, struggles with drug addiction, and a resurgence in popularity. Downey's films have grossed over $14 billion worldwide, making him one of the highest-grossing actors of all time. 

Downey was born on April 4, 1965, in Manhattan, New York City. He began acting at the age of five in his father Robert Downey Sr.'s film "Pound". After dropping out of Santa Monica High School, Downey moved to New York City to pursue his acting career. He gained recognition for his roles in films like "Less Than Zero" (1987) and "Chaplin" (1992).

Downey's personal life was marred by substance abuse issues and legal troubles throughout the 1990s. He spent time in prison and rehab, and his career was put on hold. In 2003, he made a comeback after Mel Gibson helped him get insured for the film "The Singing Detective".

Downey's career skyrocketed after he starred as Tony Stark in the "Iron Man" franchise. He played the iconic superhero in ten Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) films, becoming synonymous with the character. 

He has also appeared in other successful franchises like "Sherlock Holmes" and has starred in films such as "Tropic Thunder" (2008) and "Oppenheimer" (2023). 

Downey's performance as Lewis Strauss in "Oppenheimer" earned him critical acclaim and several awards, including an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor. 

He maintains his sobriety and has been vocal about his experiences with addiction. He is a supporter of criminal justice reform and environmentalism.  

Downey is set to return to the MCU as Victor von Doom/Doctor Doom in the upcoming "Fantastic Four" and "Avengers" films. He is also set to make his Broadway debut in 2024.
