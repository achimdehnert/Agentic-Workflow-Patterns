# Angelina Jolie: Actress, Filmmaker, and Humanitarian

Angelina Jolie is an American actress, filmmaker, and humanitarian known for her captivating performances, edgy roles, and extensive charitable work.  Born in Los Angeles on June 4, 1975, Jolie's acting career began in the early 1990s, with her first starring role in the film "Cyborg 2" (1993). [1]  She gained recognition for her roles in the television films "George Wallace" (1997) and "Gia" (1998), the latter of which earned her a Golden Globe Award. [2, 3]  Her breakthrough role came in the 1999 drama "Girl, Interrupted," for which she won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress. [1, 2, 3]

Jolie's film career saw her take on various genres, including action films like "Lara Croft: Tomb Raider" (2001), "Mr. & Mrs. Smith" (2005), "Wanted" (2008), and "Salt" (2010), which solidified her status as a Hollywood action star. [1]  She also received critical acclaim for her performances in the dramas "A Mighty Heart" (2007) and "Changeling" (2008). [1, 2, 3]  Her role in "Changeling" earned her an Academy Award nomination for Best Actress. [1] 

In 2011, Jolie made her feature directorial debut with "In the Land of Blood and Honey," a love story set during the Bosnian War.  She has since directed several other films, including "Unbroken" (2014), "By the Sea" (2015), and "First They Killed My Father" (2017). [1, 2, 3]

Beyond her acting and directing, Jolie is widely recognized for her humanitarian efforts.  In 2001, she became a Goodwill Ambassador for the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR), traveling to war zones and refugee camps around the world to raise awareness about the plight of displaced persons. [1, 2, 3] Her work with the UNHCR led to her appointment as a Special Envoy in 2012, allowing her to represent the organization on a diplomatic level. [1] She resigned from the ambassadorship in December 2022 but continues to advocate for refugees. [1]

Jolie's advocacy extends to other humanitarian causes, including conservation, education, and women's rights. She has established several foundations to support these efforts, including the Maddox Jolie-Pitt Foundation, which focuses on conservation and community development in Cambodia. [1] She has also been active in advocating for legislation to aid child immigrants and refugees in the United States. [1]

Jolie has been married three times, to Jonny Lee Miller (1996–1999), Billy Bob Thornton (2000–2003), and Brad Pitt (2014–2019). [1, 2, 3] She has six children, three of whom were adopted internationally. [1, 2, 3] Her personal life, as well as her humanitarian work, has often been subject to intense media scrutiny. [1, 2, 3]

Jolie's public image is closely tied to her perceived beauty and sex appeal.  She has been named the world's most beautiful woman and sexiest woman alive by various publications. [1] Her physical attributes have been widely imitated, and her open discussion of her preventive mastectomy and oophorectomy has sparked conversations about cancer awareness and preventative healthcare. [1]
