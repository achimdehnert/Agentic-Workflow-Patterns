# Tom Cruise: Hollywood Icon and Action Star

Tom Cruise is a renowned American actor and producer who has achieved significant success in Hollywood, becoming a global icon. [1] He is known for his charismatic personality, action-packed films, and dedication to performing his own stunts. [1, 2] His films have earned over $12 billion worldwide, solidifying his position as one of the highest-grossing actors of all time. [1, 5] 

Cruise's acting career began in the early 1980s, with breakout roles in comedies like Risky Business (1983) and action films like Top Gun (1986). [1, 28] He garnered critical acclaim for his performances in dramas such as The Color of Money (1986), Rain Man (1988), and Born on the Fourth of July (1989). [1, 31] He established himself as a leading Hollywood star in the 1990s, starring in commercially successful films like A Few Good Men (1992), The Firm (1993), Interview with the Vampire (1994), and the romance Jerry Maguire (1996). [1, 33]  

He solidified his status as an action star in the 2000s, showcasing his ability to perform risky stunts in films like Vanilla Sky (2001), Minority Report (2002), The Last Samurai (2003), Collateral (2004), War of the Worlds (2005), and the Mission: Impossible franchise which he has starred in since 1996. [1, 38, 45, 46] He has also starred in other action films like Knight and Day (2010), Jack Reacher (2012), Oblivion (2013), Edge of Tomorrow (2014), and Top Gun: Maverick (2022). [1, 51, 59, 62, 64, 80]

Cruise's personal life has been the subject of considerable media attention. He has been married three times: to Mimi Rogers, Nicole Kidman, and Katie Holmes. [2] His outspoken advocacy for Scientology has generated controversy, particularly his criticism of psychiatry and anti-depressants. [2, 165, 166] 

Cruise continues to be a prominent figure in Hollywood and has a loyal fanbase. [3] His commitment to action films and his willingness to push boundaries have made him a recognizable and respected actor. [1, 2]
