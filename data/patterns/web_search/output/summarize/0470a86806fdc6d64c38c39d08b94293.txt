# Natalie Portman: Actress, Director, and Activist

Natalie Portman is an Israeli-born American actress known for her diverse roles and activism. She began acting at age twelve, starring as Mathilda in the action film *Léon: The Professional* (1994) [1]. Portman gained international recognition for her role as Padmé Amidala in the *Star Wars* prequel trilogy (1999-2005) [1]. Despite her early success, she prioritized education, graduating from Harvard University with a degree in psychology in 2003 [1].

Portman has received numerous accolades for her acting, including an Academy Award for Best Actress for her performance in the psychological thriller *Black Swan* (2010) [2]. She has starred in various other acclaimed films, including *Closer* (2004), *V for Vendetta* (2005), *Thor* (2011), and *Jackie* (2016) [1, 2].

Beyond her acting career, Portman is a dedicated advocate for animal rights and environmental causes. She became a vegetarian at age eight and later adopted a vegan lifestyle in 2009 [1]. She is also involved in combating poverty and promoting microfinance through her work with FINCA International [1]. 

Portman is a supporter of the Democratic Party and has campaigned for various candidates, including John Kerry, Hillary Clinton, and Barack Obama [1]. She has been critical of the Israeli government and its policies, particularly those of former Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu [1].

Portman has directed two films, the short *Eve* (2008) and the biographical drama *A Tale of Love and Darkness* (2015) [1]. She also co-founded the production company MountainA in 2021, which has produced the drama *May December* (2023) and the HBO documentary series *Angel City* [1].
