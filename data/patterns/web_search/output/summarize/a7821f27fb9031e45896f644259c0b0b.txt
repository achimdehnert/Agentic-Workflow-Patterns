# Charlize Theron: Actress, Producer, Activist

Charlize Theron is a renowned South African and American actress, producer, and activist. She is known for her versatility and has received critical acclaim for her performances in various genres, including drama, action, and comedy. Her career began with modeling and dance, but a knee injury led her to pursue acting in the United States. Theron gained international prominence with her role in the film "The Devil's Advocate" (1997) and achieved critical acclaim for her portrayal of serial killer Aileen Wuornos in "Monster" (2003). 

For her performance in "Monster," Theron won an Academy Award for Best Actress, becoming the first South African to win an acting Oscar. She has since starred in a wide range of films, including "The Italian Job" (2003), "Hancock" (2008), "Snow White and the Huntsman" (2012), "Mad Max: Fury Road" (2015), "Atomic Blonde" (2017), and "The Old Guard" (2020). She has also ventured into film production with her company Denver and Delilah Productions and has produced numerous films, including "The Burning Plain" (2008) and "Long Shot" (2019).

Theron is also a vocal advocate for social causes and has been recognized for her humanitarian efforts. She is a United Nations Messenger of Peace and founded the Charlize Theron Africa Outreach Project (CTAOP) in 2007 to support African youth in the fight against HIV/AIDS. She is also a strong supporter of women's rights and same-sex marriage.
