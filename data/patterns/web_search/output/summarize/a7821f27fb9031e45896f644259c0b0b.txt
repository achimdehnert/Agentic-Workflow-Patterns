# Charlize Theron: A South African Actress with International Acclaim

Charlize Theron is a highly acclaimed South African and American actress and producer. She has achieved global recognition for her versatile acting abilities and has earned numerous accolades, including an Academy Award, a Golden Globe Award, and a Screen Actors Guild Award. 

Theron's career began in the late 1990s with roles in films like "The Devil's Advocate" (1997) and "The Cider House Rules" (1999).  However, her breakthrough role came in 2003 with her portrayal of serial killer Aileen Wuornos in "Monster," for which she won the Academy Award for Best Actress, making her the first South African to win an acting Oscar. She has also starred in several commercially successful action films, including "The Italian Job" (2003), "Hancock" (2008), "Mad Max: Fury Road" (2015), and "Atomic Blonde" (2017). 

Theron's filmography also includes critically acclaimed roles in comedy-dramas like "Young Adult" (2011) and "Tully" (2018), as well as the biographical drama "Bombshell" (2019), which earned her a third Academy Award nomination. 

Beyond acting, Theron is actively involved in activism. She founded the Charlize Theron Africa Outreach Project (CTAOP) in 2007 to support African youth in the fight against HIV/AIDS. She is a United Nations Messenger of Peace and a vocal supporter of women's rights. 

Theron became an American citizen in 2007 while retaining her South African citizenship. She has adopted two daughters and is known for her dedication to her craft, her outspoken nature, and her philanthropic endeavors.
