# Tom Hanks: An American Icon

Tom Hanks is an American actor, filmmaker, and cultural icon known for his comedic and dramatic roles. His films have grossed over $9.96 billion worldwide, making him the fourth-highest-grossing American actor.  Hanks's breakout roles came in comedies like *Splash* (1984) and *Big* (1988). He won two consecutive Academy Awards for Best Actor for his roles in *Philadelphia* (1993) and *Forrest Gump* (1994). 

Hanks has collaborated with numerous renowned directors, including Steven Spielberg, Ron Howard, Nora Ephron, and Robert Zemeckis. He has starred in a wide range of films across genres, including romantic comedies like *Sleepless in Seattle* (1993) and *You've Got Mail* (1998), dramas like *Apollo 13* (1995), *The Green Mile* (1999), *Cast Away* (2000), and *Cloud Atlas* (2012), and biographical dramas such as *Charlie Wilson's War* (2007), *Captain Phillips* (2013), *Saving Mr. Banks* (2013), *Sully* (2016), and *A Beautiful Day in the Neighborhood* (2019). Hanks is also known for voicing Sheriff Woody in the *Toy Story* franchise (1995-2019).

Beyond acting, Hanks has also directed comedies, including *That Thing You Do!* (1996) and *Larry Crowne* (2011), and produced numerous films and television miniseries, such as *From the Earth to the Moon* (1998), *Band of Brothers* (2001), and *The Pacific* (2010). In 2013, he made his Broadway debut in *Lucky Guy*, earning a Tony Award nomination. 

He has received numerous honors, including the American Film Institute's Life Achievement Award (2002), the Kennedy Center Honor (2014), the Presidential Medal of Freedom (2016), and the Golden Globe Cecil B. DeMille Award (2020).

Hanks's personal life is marked by his two marriages, first to Samantha Lewes and then to Rita Wilson. He has four children. His career success, combined with his philanthropic efforts, has led to him being widely recognized as a respected and beloved figure in American culture.
