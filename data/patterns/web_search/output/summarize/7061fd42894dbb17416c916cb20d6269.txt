# Brad Pitt: A Hollywood Icon

Brad Pitt, born William Bradley Pitt on December 18, 1963, is a renowned American actor and film producer. He has gained worldwide recognition for his captivating performances in numerous films, including "Fight Club," "The Curious Case of Benjamin Button," and "Once Upon a Time... in Hollywood," which earned him an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor.  Pitt's career began with minor television roles before his breakthrough performance as a charming hitchhiker in "Thelma & Louise" (1991). He has since cemented his status as a Hollywood leading man, appearing in blockbuster films like "Troy" (2004) and "Mr. & Mrs. Smith" (2005). Beyond acting, Pitt is a successful film producer, having co-founded Plan B Entertainment in 2001, which has produced several critically acclaimed films, including the Academy Award-winning "12 Years a Slave" (2013). 

Pitt's personal life has also been a subject of intense media scrutiny, particularly his high-profile marriages to actresses Jennifer Aniston and Angelina Jolie. He has six children with Jolie: Maddox, Pax, Zahara, Shiloh, Knox, and Vivienne.  Throughout his career, Pitt has been actively involved in various humanitarian causes, co-founding the "Not On Our Watch" campaign and establishing the "Make It Right Foundation," a project to build environmentally friendly homes in New Orleans.
