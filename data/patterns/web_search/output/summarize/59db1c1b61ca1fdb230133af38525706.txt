# Jennifer Lawrence: A Multifaceted Actress

Jennifer Lawrence is an American actress and producer, known for her versatility in both action film franchises and independent dramas.  Her films have grossed over $6 billion worldwide. [1]  Lawrence was born in 1990 in Kentucky and began acting as a child in church plays and school musicals. [1]  At age 14, she was discovered by a talent scout while on vacation in New York City and began pursuing acting professionally. [1]

Lawrence's breakthrough role came in 2010 with the independent drama "Winter's Bone," for which she received her first Oscar nomination. [1] She went on to achieve global stardom with her portrayal of Katniss Everdeen in "The Hunger Games" film series (2012â€“2015), making her the highest-grossing action heroine of all time. [1]  She also collaborated with filmmaker David O. Russell on three films, earning various accolades. [1]  She won the Academy Award for Best Actress for her role in the romantic comedy-drama "Silver Linings Playbook" (2012), becoming the second-youngest winner in the category. [1]  Lawrence is a feminist and advocates for women's reproductive rights. [1]  In 2015, she founded the Jennifer Lawrence Foundation, which supports charities such as the Boys & Girls Clubs of America and the Special Olympics. [1]

Lawrence's career has seen fluctuations, with a series of mixed reviewed films leading to a small break from acting in the late 2010s. [1]  She returned to film in 2021 with the black comedy "Don't Look Up" and has since produced and starred in the drama "Causeway" (2022) and the comedy "No Hard Feelings" (2023). [1]
