# Cate Blanchett: A Celebrated Actress

Cate Blanchett is an Australian actress and film producer renowned for her versatility and impactful performances across a wide range of genres.  Blanchett's career began on the Australian stage, gaining early recognition for her roles in productions like "Oleanna" and "Electra." [1] Her international breakthrough came in 1998 with her portrayal of Queen Elizabeth I in the film "Elizabeth," which earned her an Academy Award nomination. [1, 2]

Blanchett has received numerous awards and nominations throughout her career, including two Academy Awards, four British Academy Film Awards, and four Golden Globe Awards. [1] She won her first Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress for her portrayal of Katharine Hepburn in Martin Scorsese's "The Aviator" (2004). [1] She later won the Academy Award for Best Actress for her performance in Woody Allen's "Blue Jasmine" (2013), playing a neurotic former socialite. [1]  

Blanchett's filmography includes a diverse array of notable films, including "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy (2001-2003), "Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull" (2008), "The Curious Case of Benjamin Button" (2008), "Cinderella" (2015), "Thor: Ragnarok" (2017), "Ocean's 8" (2018), and "Don't Look Up" (2021). [1]  

In addition to her film work, Blanchett has also been a prominent figure in theatre. She and her husband, Andrew Upton, served as artistic directors of the Sydney Theatre Company from 2008 to 2013. [1] She made her Broadway debut in 2017 in "The Present," earning a nomination for the Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play. [1]

Beyond her acting career, Blanchett is known for her activism in environmental and humanitarian causes. [1] She has been a long-time advocate for climate change action and has served as a global Goodwill Ambassador for the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) since 2015. [1]
