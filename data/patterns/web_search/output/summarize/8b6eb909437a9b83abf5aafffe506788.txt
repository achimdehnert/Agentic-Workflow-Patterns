# Jennifer Lawrence: Actress, Producer, and Activist

Jennifer Lawrence is an American actress and producer known for her versatility in both action film franchises and independent dramas. She is one of the world’s highest-paid actresses and has received critical acclaim for her performances, including an Academy Award for Best Actress for her role in *Silver Linings Playbook* (2012).  Lawrence has also been recognized for her advocacy work, supporting causes such as women’s reproductive rights, and promoting body positivity. 

Lawrence began her career as a teenager with guest roles on television, including a starring role in the sitcom *The Bill Engvall Show* (2007–2009).  Her breakout role was in *Winter’s Bone* (2010), for which she received an Academy Award nomination. She rose to greater fame portraying Mystique in the *X-Men* film series (2011–2019) and Katniss Everdeen in *The Hunger Games* film series (2012–2015). 

She has also collaborated with filmmaker David O. Russell on three films, earning various accolades.  For her portrayal of Tiffany Maxwell in *Silver Linings Playbook*, she won the Academy Award for Best Actress, becoming the second-youngest winner in the category. She won the BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role for *American Hustle* (2013), and received Golden Globe Awards for both films, as well as for her performance as businesswoman Joy Mangano in the biopic *Joy* (2015). 

Lawrence founded the Jennifer Lawrence Foundation in 2015, which supports charities such as the Boys & Girls Clubs of America and the Special Olympics. She is also a vocal feminist and advocates for women’s reproductive rights. In 2015, she wrote an essay criticizing the gender pay gap in Hollywood, highlighting her own experiences in the industry.

In 2019, Lawrence married Cooke Maroney, an art dealer. The couple welcomed their first child, a son named Cy, in 2022.
