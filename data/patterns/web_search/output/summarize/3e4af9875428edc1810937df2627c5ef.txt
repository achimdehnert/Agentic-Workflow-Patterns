# Leonardo DiCaprio: A Cinematic Icon and Environmental Advocate 

Leonardo DiCaprio is an American actor and film producer whose work spans across biographical, period, and action thriller films.  DiCaprio's career has been marked by critical acclaim and widespread commercial success, grossing over $7.2 billion worldwide. He is a recipient of numerous awards, including an Academy Award, a British Academy Film Award, and three Golden Globe Awards. 

DiCaprio's acting career began in the late 1980s, starting with television commercials.  He rose to prominence in the early 1990s with roles in television shows like "Parenthood" and "Growing Pains," and his film breakthrough came with his role in "What's Eating Gilbert Grape" (1993), earning him an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor. 

DiCaprio achieved international stardom with the 1996 film "Romeo + Juliet" and the blockbuster "Titanic" (1997), solidifying his status as a teen idol.  After "Titanic," he focused on roles in other genres, collaborating with renowned directors like Martin Scorsese on films such as "Gangs of New York" (2002), "The Aviator" (2004), "The Departed" (2006), "Shutter Island" (2010), "The Wolf of Wall Street" (2013), and "Killers of the Flower Moon" (2023). 

DiCaprio has a long-standing commitment to environmental activism, founding the Leonardo DiCaprio Foundation, a non-profit organization dedicated to promoting environmental awareness. He was named a United Nations Messenger of Peace in 2014, focusing on climate change.

DiCaprio has starred in a wide range of films, including "The Beach" (2000), "Catch Me If You Can" (2002), "Blood Diamond" (2006), "Inception" (2010), "Django Unchained" (2012), "The Great Gatsby" (2013), "The Revenant" (2015), "Once Upon a Time in Hollywood" (2019), "Don't Look Up" (2021), and "Killers of the Flower Moon" (2023). 

He is known for his dedication to his craft, immersing himself in roles and collaborating with some of the most respected directors in the industry.  DiCaprio's commitment to environmental issues has made him a leading voice in the climate change movement.
